Phase 0: Stop the Bleeding — Days 1–3

Before the 90-day plan, before TikTok Shop, before grant applications — there are three things that either buy you time or tell you whether you have any. These are not Week 1 tasks. They are Day 1 tasks. None of them cost money.

Day 1
Call your landlord
Request a meeting. Ask about rent deferral, reduced rent for 60–90 days, or conversion to a percentage-of-revenue structure. Landlords in commercial retail know the alternative is a vacant unit — you have more leverage than you think. If your lease is up for renewal, that's more leverage. If they say no, you know your number. If they say yes, you bought time.
Day 1–2
Launch a GoFundMe or Ko-fi
You have 270K TikTok followers who just watched a video about your business struggling. A "help keep this queer-owned shop alive" campaign framed around aspiration (not desperation) could generate $2,000–$10,000 in under two weeks. GoFundMe has zero fees for personal/business campaigns. Ko-fi lets supporters give any amount with no platform cut. This is not charity — every campaign is a gift card pre-sale or a "keep making these" contribution. Post one video to TikTok and pin it.
Day 2–3
Apply to Kiva
Kiva is a crowdfunded microloan platform: 0% interest, up to $15,000, specifically designed for underserved entrepreneurs. LGBTQ+ and women-owned businesses have high approval rates. The application is straightforward — business description, how you'll use the funds, financial snapshot. Not bank-grade underwriting. Approval and funding typically takes 2–4 weeks. Go to kiva.org/borrow to start. This is not a grant but it's the cheapest debt available.
Day 3
Check Square Capital eligibility
If you process a meaningful volume through Square, you may already have a pre-qualified offer waiting. Log into your Square Dashboard → Capital → Check for offers. Square Capital advances are repaid as a percentage of daily sales — no fixed monthly payment. The cost is higher than a traditional loan (factor rate typically 1.1–1.3x) but the availability is instant and the repayment is tied to your revenue. Check before you need it.
Why this comes before everything else: Grants take 30–120 days from application to cash. SEO takes months. TikTok Shop takes 1–3 days to approve and longer to build revenue. None of these save you this week. A landlord conversation, a GoFundMe, and a Kiva application can. Do Phase 0 first, then run the 90-day plan in parallel.

Security Audit — 5 Issues, 1 Plugin Fixes Most of Them

Your payments are secure (Square handles all card processing — your site never touches credit card data). But your WordPress installation has five issues that could let someone take over your site or impersonate your email address. All are fixable free.

The One Fix That Covers Most of This

Install Wordfence Security (free WordPress plugin) — it blocks brute force attacks, disables XML-RPC, hides staff usernames, and adds firewall protection. Takes 5 minutes. Login to WordPress → Plugins → Add New → search "Wordfence" → Install → Activate. The two email issues require separate DNS changes (instructions below).

CRITICAL

No DMARC Record — Anyone Can Fake Your Email Free Fix

Without a DMARC DNS record, anyone on the internet can send email that appears to come from hello@thetoastedmallow.com. Spammers and scammers use this to impersonate businesses, and it damages your email deliverability with providers like Gmail.

In your Bluehost DNS panel, add a TXT record: Host: _dmarc / Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:hello@thetoastedmallow.com. Free, takes effect in 24-48 hours.
CRITICAL

Two Conflicting SPF Records Break Email Delivery Free Fix

You have two SPF records, which is invalid — email servers only accept one. This causes some of your legitimate outbound emails (order confirmations, marketing) to be marked as spam or rejected entirely.

In Bluehost DNS, delete one SPF record and keep the other. If unsure which to keep, ask Bluehost support — they can identify which record covers your email provider. Free, 10 minutes.
HIGH

WordPress Login Has No Brute Force Protection Free Fix

Your WordPress admin login (/wp-admin) is accessible from anywhere with no rate limiting. Automated bots continuously attempt logins on exposed WordPress sites — this is not hypothetical, it's happening now on almost every public WordPress installation.

Wordfence (free) enables login limiting automatically on install. Set it to lock out after 5 failed attempts for 1 hour.
HIGH

XML-RPC Enabled — Remote Attack Vector Free Fix

XML-RPC is a legacy WordPress feature that's almost never used by modern sites. It allows attackers to attempt thousands of password guesses in a single request, bypassing normal login rate limits.

Wordfence disables XML-RPC automatically. Or disable manually in Wordfence settings → Firewall → Block XML-RPC requests.

Legal & Compliance Checklist — Before You Scale

You operate a licensed commercial kitchen, which puts you above cottage food territory — but expanding online and nationally adds a short checklist. None of these are reasons to delay. They're each a quick call or one-time setup.

AZ Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)
If you're selling taxable goods in Arizona (retail food is generally exempt, but check your specific product mix), you need an Arizona TPT license. Online sales to AZ customers may also apply. Free to register at azdor.gov.
FDA Nutrition Labeling
Packaged food sold nationally requires an FDA-compliant label: ingredient list, nutrition facts panel, allergen declarations, net weight, and manufacturer name/address. This is also required for TikTok Shop. Software like Genesis R&D or Nutritionix can generate panels from your recipes.
Product Liability Insurance
General liability covers your physical location. As you ship nationally, a product liability rider or separate policy protects against a customer allergic reaction or injury claim. Cost: typically $500–$1,500/yr for a small food business. Required by most wholesale buyers (Faire, larger cafes).
AZ Cottage Food Law (Context Only)
AZ cottage food law allows unlicensed home production up to $75K/yr — but since you operate a licensed commercial kitchen, this doesn't apply to you. It's useful context if you hire a helper or co-manufacturer who produces from home.
GoFundMe / Ko-fi Income Is Taxable
Crowdfunding income received for a business purpose is generally taxable as business income. Track all amounts received. If you pursue a campaign seriously, set aside 15–25% for self-employment tax. Platforms issue 1099-K if you exceed $600 in a calendar year (2024+ IRS threshold).

Most states allow packaged confections to ship freely. A few states have specific requirements for food shipped in. In-Person Sales

Events and Markets — What's Worth Doing

Not all events are created equal. The Toasted Mallow has appeared at events that reviewers called out as highlights — and some that weren't worth the setup. The difference isn't random. Here's the framework for choosing and maximizing in-person sales.

Why Some Events Don't Pay Off

The most common cause of a bad event: the audience came for something else. Artisan marshmallows are an impulse buy — visitors need to be in a buying mindset, not just browsing. Generic craft fairs, art shows, and community fairs where food isn't the draw consistently underperform. High booth fees ($300+) at low-foot-traffic events kill the math even when sales are decent. And in Phoenix, outdoor summer events with heat above 95°F are a product risk — marshmallows don't hold up.

Event Tier Ranking

Event Type Tier Why It Works (or Doesn't) Watch For
Holiday gift markets (Nov–Dec) TIER 1 Buyers arrive with wallets open specifically to buy gifts. Artisan food is a top gifting category. Your product boxes beautifully. Average transaction is highest here. Booth fees spike in Nov–Dec. Negotiate early or look for non-profit/community markets with lower fees.
Artisan food festivals TIER 1 Audience self-selected for food interest. High impulse conversion when sampling is allowed. Your product has visual appeal that wins in a food crowd. Watch outdoor heat windows (Oct–April in AZ). Summer food festivals are hard unless indoor/shaded.

Online follow-up conversion is higher from this audience. Phoenix Pride in April is the main anchor. Tempe, Mesa, and Tucson have smaller events worth evaluating by booth cost vs. expected traffic.

Requires a branded favor sample kit at the booth. Lead time is long — weddings book 6–18 months out. Bring a sign-up form for "wedding consult" rather than trying to close same-day.

Avoid markets more than 20 minutes away — the labor math breaks unless volume is very high.

This is AZ-specific — skip summer outdoor events entirely. Exception: shaded or fully indoor events with AC even in summer months are fine. Verify venue conditions before committing.

The Break-Even Math

Before committing to any event, run this quick check:

Booth fee: $[X]
Staff (hours x rate): $[Y]
Product cost at cost: $[Z]
Total out: $[X+Y+Z]
Avg transaction: $[T]
Break-even sales needed: (X+Y+Z) / T

If break-even requires more than 40% of expected foot traffic to buy, the event is too risky at that booth fee.

Booth Setup That Converts

  • Samples are non-negotiable — taste converts faster than any sign
  • Height variation: elevate product at different levels so the table reads as abundant, not flat
  • Bundle pricing at events: "3 bags for $X" increases avg transaction by 40–60%
  • Pre-made gift sets in clear bags with ribbon — no decision-making required, impulse-buy format
  • A single clear price sign per product — decision paralysis at events kills sales
  • Cash and tap-to-pay both available — lost sales from "cash only" are real

Converting Visitors to Online Customers

The event sale is not the end of the transaction — it's the beginning of a customer relationship.

  • QR code on every bag and on the table: goes directly to online shop or email signup
  • Insert card in every purchase: "Ships nationwide — send a box to someone you love" with URL
  • Text/email signup at the booth: "10% off your next online order" in exchange for their info
  • TikTok handle on all signage — event visitors who follow you see every future video
  • Film the event for TikTok content: setup, samples, crowd reactions, "pack an order" at the booth

Events as Content, Not Just Revenue

Every event is a TikTok video. Booth setup time-lapses, sample reactions, the crowd, the behind-the-scenes of packing and unpacking — this content consistently performs. A single well-filmed event appearance can drive more online sales than the event itself. Assign one person to be product/customer-facing and one to document. If there's only one person, film the setup before it gets busy.
