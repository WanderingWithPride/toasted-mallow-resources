Strategic Assessment

Three Paths Forward

You're facing three options. Each one touches the problem differently. Here's what the numbers actually say about each — revenue impact, overhead reduction, risk, and what signal it sends to your customers.

Option A — Close 3 Days/Week

Reduce operating hours to cut staff and supply costs without changing the product mix or locations.

Revenue ImpactNegative — loses revenue
Overhead CutMinimal — fixed costs stay
RiskHigh
Customer Signal"We're struggling"

Option B — Drop Cupcakes

Eliminate the cupcake line. Improves margins at the product level but doesn't address fixed overhead.

Revenue ImpactModest improvement
Overhead CutLow — rent/utilities stay
RiskLow
Bottom LineHelpful, not sufficient

Option C — E-Commerce Pivot

Go online-only for marshmallows nationally. Keep milkshakes in-store. Consolidate to one location.

Revenue ImpactHigh — opens national market
Overhead Cut~50% (one lease)
RiskMedium — transition period
Customer SignalEvolution, not retreat

The Verdict: Option C + B — together

Option B (drop cupcakes) is necessary but not enough on its own. Option C (e-commerce pivot + consolidate to one location) is what actually solves the problem. Combined, they cut fixed overhead by ~50% — one lease, one set of utilities, fewer staff — while opening the national market for marshmallows. Option A is the worst path: it signals decline to customers, reduces revenue more than it reduces costs, and doesn't touch the real problem.

The precedent you need to know: XO Marshmallow (Chicago) ran the world's first marshmallow cafe — same category as you. Their cafe closed in 2024. Their e-commerce immediately jumped 325% and they became a seven-figure business. TikTok Shop alone projects over $1M in revenue for them. You have something XO didn't: 10+ years of history, two locations as proof of scale, verified regional TV features (Arizona PBS, CBS 5, FOX 10 Phoenix), and dual certification (LGBTQ+ + women-owned) that opens major grant programs to you.
On consolidation: Don't close both locations — close one. The remaining location becomes your marshmallow fulfillment hub and milkshake bar. In-person milkshake revenue is high-impulse and keeps the brand visible locally while e-commerce scales nationally. The physical space also serves as your content studio for TikTok production.
Before committing to consolidation — check these first:
Lease exit cost: Early termination in AZ typically carries a 2–6 month rent penalty. If your second location has 12+ months left on the lease, the break cost changes the math significantly — find out the exact figure before making this call.

Staff impact: Consolidation likely means reducing headcount. Budget time and emotional bandwidth for that conversation. At under 10 employees you're below WARN Act thresholds, but how you handle it will affect your reputation in the local community and your ability to hire again.

Website Security

Security Audit — 5 Issues, 1 Plugin Fixes Most of Them

Your payments are secure (Square handles all card processing — your site never touches credit card data). But your WordPress installation has five issues that could let someone take over your site or impersonate your email address. All are fixable free.

The One Fix That Covers Most of This

Install Wordfence Security (free WordPress plugin) — it blocks brute force attacks, disables XML-RPC, hides staff usernames, and adds firewall protection. Takes 5 minutes. Login to WordPress → Plugins → Add New → search "Wordfence" → Install → Activate. The two email issues require separate DNS changes (instructions below).

⚠️

No DMARC Record — Anyone Can Fake Your Email Free Fix

Without a DMARC DNS record, anyone on the internet can send email that appears to come from hello@thetoastedmallow.com. Spammers and scammers use this to impersonate businesses, and it damages your email deliverability with providers like Gmail.

In your Bluehost DNS panel, add a TXT record: Host: _dmarc / Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:hello@thetoastedmallow.com. Free, takes effect in 24-48 hours.
⚠️

Two Conflicting SPF Records Break Email Delivery Free Fix

You have two SPF records, which is invalid — email servers only accept one. This causes some of your legitimate outbound emails (order confirmations, marketing) to be marked as spam or rejected entirely.

In Bluehost DNS, delete one SPF record and keep the other. If unsure which to keep, ask Bluehost support — they can identify which record covers your email provider. Free, 10 minutes.
🔒

WordPress Login Has No Brute Force Protection Free Fix

Your WordPress admin login (/wp-admin) is accessible from anywhere with no rate limiting. Automated bots continuously attempt logins on exposed WordPress sites — this is not hypothetical, it's happening now on almost every public WordPress installation.

Wordfence (free) enables login limiting automatically on install. Set it to lock out after 5 failed attempts for 1 hour.
🔒

XML-RPC Enabled — Remote Attack Vector Free Fix

XML-RPC is a legacy WordPress feature that's almost never used by modern sites. It allows attackers to attempt thousands of password guesses in a single request, bypassing normal login rate limits.

Wordfence disables XML-RPC automatically. Or disable manually in Wordfence settings → Firewall → Block XML-RPC requests.
👤

Staff Usernames Publicly Visible Free Fix

WordPress's REST API exposes all user login names at a public URL. Attackers use these as the "username" half of a brute force attempt — they already have the username, they just need to guess the password.

Wordfence blocks this endpoint by default. Or go to Wordfence → Tools → Diagnostics → Disable User Enumeration.
✅

SSL and Payments Are Secure

Your SSL certificate (Let's Encrypt) is valid and auto-renews. TLS 1.3 is active. Your Square Online shop (shop.thetoastedmallow.com) is separate — Square processes all payments on their infrastructure. Your WordPress site never handles payment data.

No action needed. Domain expires 2027 — verify auto-renew is on in your Bluehost account to avoid accidental expiry.

Three Structural Moves That Compound Over Time

  • Consolidate to 2 primary suppliers. Spreading purchases across 5+ suppliers eliminates your volume leverage. Concentrating gelatin + sugar + syrup with Bakers Authority and Stover builds order history that earns better pricing and net-30 terms.
  • Pre-buy vanilla and seasonal flavoring extracts. Natural vanilla has significant price volatility. Buying a 3–6 month supply when prices are low is standard practice among confectioners. Ask your current supplier for price history or watch commodity reports.
  • Connect with 2–3 other Phoenix artisan food makers. A chocolatier, a caramel shop, and a cookie bakery sharing an order from Bakers Authority or Stover can hit volume thresholds that trigger better pricing for everyone — without any of you needing pallet-level quantities alone.

In-Person Sales

Events and Markets — What's Worth Doing

Not all events are created equal. The Toasted Mallow has appeared at events that reviewers called out as highlights — and some that weren't worth the setup. The difference isn't random. Here's the framework for choosing and maximizing in-person sales.

Why Some Events Don't Pay Off

The most common cause of a bad event: the audience came for something else. Artisan marshmallows are an impulse buy — visitors need to be in a buying mindset, not just browsing. Generic craft fairs, art shows, and community fairs where food isn't the draw consistently underperform. High booth fees ($300+) at low-foot-traffic events kill the math even when sales are decent. And in Phoenix, outdoor summer events with heat above 95°F are a product risk — marshmallows don't hold up.

Event Tier Ranking

Event Type Tier Why It Works (or Doesn't) Watch For
Holiday gift markets (Nov–Dec) TIER 1 Buyers arrive with wallets open specifically to buy gifts. Artisan food is a top gifting category. Your product boxes beautifully. Average transaction is highest here. Booth fees spike in Nov–Dec. Negotiate early or look for non-profit/community markets with lower fees.
Artisan food festivals TIER 1 Audience self-selected for food interest. High impulse conversion when sampling is allowed. Your product has visual appeal that wins in a food crowd. Watch outdoor heat windows (Oct–April in AZ). Summer food festivals are hard unless indoor/shaded.
LGBTQ+ Pride events TIER 1 Community identity match. LGBTQ+-owned business at a Pride event gets loyalty and story-driven purchases, not just impulse. Online follow-up conversion is higher from this audience. Phoenix Pride in April is the main anchor. Tempe, Mesa, and Tucson have smaller events worth evaluating by booth cost vs. expected traffic.
Bridal expos / wedding fairs TIER 2 Custom marshmallow favors and dessert table add-ons are an underserved niche. High average order potential ($200–$1,000+ per wedding booking). Requires a branded favor sample kit at the booth. Lead time is long — weddings book 6–18 months out. Bring a sign-up form for "wedding consult" rather than trying to close same-day.
Farmers markets (recurring) TIER 2 The value is the repeat customer, not the one-time sale. A loyal farmers market customer who buys a $20 bag every two weeks is worth $500/year. Takes 4–6 weeks to build regulars. Don't evaluate a farmers market on week one. Gilbert Farmers Market and Chandler-area markets are the obvious first targets. Avoid markets more than 20 minutes away — the labor math breaks unless volume is very high.
Corporate vendor fairs TIER 2 HR departments host internal vendor fairs for holiday gifting. One corporate account found here can be worth $2,000–$10,000 in bulk orders. The event itself may break even — the follow-up is where the value is. Bring a corporate gifting menu (pricing tiers, custom branding options) and collect business cards. The sale doesn't happen at the booth.
Generic craft fairs / art shows TIER 3 Audience is browsing handmade goods, not specifically food. Conversion rate is lower. Can still work if booth fee is under $75 and setup is quick — but should not be the primary event strategy. Only worth doing if it's local (under 15 min away), fee is low, and it can be staffed without pulling from a primary shift.
Outdoor events, summer (June–Sept AZ) AVOID Product integrity risk at 100°F+. Marshmallows soften, packaging can sweat. Even if you manage product with a cooler, the customer experience suffers. This is AZ-specific — skip summer outdoor events entirely. Exception: shaded or fully indoor events with AC even in summer months are fine. Verify venue conditions before committing.

The Break-Even Math

Before committing to any event, run this quick check:

Booth fee: $[X]
Staff (hours x rate): $[Y]
Product cost at cost: $[Z]
Total out: $[X+Y+Z]
Avg transaction: $[T]
Break-even sales needed: (X+Y+Z) / T

If break-even requires more than 40% of expected foot traffic to buy, the event is too risky at that booth fee.

Booth Setup That Converts

  • Samples are non-negotiable — taste converts faster than any sign
  • Height variation: elevate product at different levels so the table reads as abundant, not flat
  • Bundle pricing at events: "3 bags for $X" increases avg transaction by 40–60%
  • Pre-made gift sets in clear bags with ribbon — no decision-making required, impulse-buy format
  • A single clear price sign per product — decision paralysis at events kills sales
  • Cash and tap-to-pay both available — lost sales from "cash only" are real

Converting Visitors to Online Customers

The event sale is not the end of the transaction — it's the beginning of a customer relationship.

  • QR code on every bag and on the table: goes directly to online shop or email signup
  • Insert card in every purchase: "Ships nationwide — send a box to someone you love" with URL
  • Text/email signup at the booth: "10% off your next online order" in exchange for their info
  • TikTok handle on all signage — event visitors who follow you see every future video
  • Film the event for TikTok content: setup, samples, crowd reactions, "pack an order" at the booth

Events as Content, Not Just Revenue

Every event is a TikTok video. Booth setup time-lapses, sample reactions, the crowd, the behind-the-scenes of packing and unpacking — this content consistently performs. A single well-filmed event appearance can drive more online sales than the event itself. Assign one person to be product/customer-facing and one to document. If there's only one person, film the setup before it gets busy.
