Strategic Assessment

Three Paths Forward

You're facing three options. Each one touches the problem differently. Here's what the numbers actually say about each — revenue impact, overhead reduction, risk, and what signal it sends to your customers.

Option A — Close 3 Days/Week

Reduce operating hours to cut staff and supply costs without changing the product mix or locations.

Revenue ImpactNegative — loses revenue
Overhead CutMinimal — fixed costs stay
RiskHigh
Customer Signal"We're struggling"

Option B — Drop Cupcakes

Eliminate the cupcake line. Improves margins at the product level but doesn't address fixed overhead.

Revenue ImpactModest improvement
Overhead CutLow — rent/utilities stay
RiskLow
Bottom LineHelpful, not sufficient

Option C — E-Commerce Pivot

Go online-only for marshmallows nationally. Keep milkshakes in-store. Consolidate to one location.

Revenue ImpactHigh — opens national market
Overhead Cut~50% (one lease)
RiskMedium — transition period
Customer SignalEvolution, not retreat

The Verdict: Option C + B — together

Option B (drop cupcakes) is necessary but not enough on its own. Option C (e-commerce pivot + consolidate to one location) is what actually solves the problem. Combined, they cut fixed overhead by ~50% — one lease, one set of utilities, fewer staff — while opening the national market for marshmallows. Option A is the worst path: it signals decline to customers, reduces revenue more than it reduces costs, and doesn't touch the real problem.

The precedent you need to know: XO Marshmallow (Chicago) ran the world's first marshmallow cafe — same category as you. Their cafe closed in 2024. Their e-commerce immediately jumped 325% and they became a seven-figure business. TikTok Shop alone projects over $1M in revenue for them. You have something XO didn't: 10+ years of history, two locations as proof of scale, Food Network/Cooking Channel credibility, and triple minority certification that opens every major grant program to you.
On consolidation: Don't close both locations — close one. The remaining location becomes your marshmallow fulfillment hub and milkshake bar. In-person milkshake revenue is high-impulse and keeps the brand visible locally while e-commerce scales nationally. The physical space also serves as your content studio for TikTok production.
Before committing to consolidation — check these first:
Lease exit cost: Early termination in AZ typically carries a 2–6 month rent penalty. If your second location has 12+ months left on the lease, the break cost changes the math significantly — find out the exact figure before making this call.

Staff impact: Consolidation likely means reducing headcount. Budget time and emotional bandwidth for that conversation. At under 10 employees you're below WARN Act thresholds, but how you handle it will affect your reputation in the local community and your ability to hire again.

Website

Site Audit — What's Broken and How to Fix It

Your Square shop already works. These fixes don't require a platform change — just configuration updates, most of which are free and can be done this week.

Critical — Fix This Week (Free)
⚠️

Stale Announcement Bar Free Fix

The current bar says "Order by 3/23 for orders to arrive in time for Easter." Easter has passed. This tells every visitor the site isn't maintained — it's the first thing they see.

Replace with: "Ships nationwide — order by Monday for delivery by Friday."
📦

Nationwide Shipping Is Hidden Free Fix

Nationwide shipping only appears after clicking ORDER NOW → popup modal. It's your most powerful selling point for an artisan food brand. It should be impossible to miss — not gated behind a click.

Add to announcement bar, hero section, and navigation. Costs nothing to add.
⭐

No Reviews on Product Pages Free Fix

You have 689 Yelp reviews at 4.7 stars and 1,722 Facebook reviews at 98% recommend. Zero of this appears anywhere on the product pages. Social proof at this scale is rare for an artisan shop.

Add a "Rated 4.7 stars by 689 customers" badge and a quote callout to every product page.
📧

No Email Capture Incentive Free Fix

The footer email form offers nothing in exchange for a signup. A list of past customers is one of the most valuable assets a food business can have — especially for launching new products and subscription sales.

Add "10% off your first order" or "free shipping on first order" to the email form. This one change will 3–5x your signup rate.
🛒

Checkout Is 5 Clicks Deep

Current path: Homepage → ORDER NOW → popup → shop subdomain → product → add to cart → checkout. That's two unnecessary clicks before a customer can even see what you sell.

Add a direct "Shop Online" link in the main navigation — bypasses the popup entirely.

Keep Customers Coming Back

Customer Retention System

Acquiring a new customer costs 5–7x more than keeping an existing one. Your repeat buyers are your most valuable asset. Right now there's no system capturing them. Here's the full stack to build it.

Email Automation · Klaviyo (Free to 500 contacts)

Email Sequences to Build

Welcome Series (3 emails): Email 1: welcome + your story. Email 2 (day 3): bestsellers + what makes your marshmallows different. Email 3 (day 7): subscription offer + first-order discount.
Abandoned Cart (3 emails): 1 hour after abandonment. 24 hours. 72 hours with 10% off. Klaviyo automates all of this — you set it up once.
Post-Purchase Flow: Thank you + order confirmation. 2 weeks later: "How are your marshmallows?" + upsell bundle. 30 days: loyalty program invite.
Win-Back (90 days no purchase): "We miss you" + new flavor drop + 15% off to bring them back.
Klaviyo integrates directly with Square Online. Free up to 500 contacts, $20/mo after that.
SMS Marketing · Postscript or Attentive

The Highest Open-Rate Channel You're Not Using

Email open rates: 20–30%. SMS open rates: 98%. For food brands with a loyal customer base, SMS is the single highest-converting marketing channel available. Use it sparingly — 2–4 messages per month maximum.

Flash sale: "48-hour sale on s'mores kits — link in bio and here: [link]"
New flavor drop: "New flavor just dropped — first 50 orders get free shipping"
Subscription renewal nudge: "Your Mallow of the Month is ready to ship — reply YES to confirm"
Seasonal: "Holiday gift sets are live — custom boxes ship through Dec 18"
Postscript integrates with Square. Free trial, then ~$25/mo for small lists.
Loyalty & Referrals · Smile.io (Free Tier)

Turn Customers Into Ambassadors

Smile.io works with Square and has a free plan. It takes 1–2 hours to configure and runs automatically after that.

Points system: 1 point per $1 spent. 100 points = $5 off. Reward reviews, social shares, birthdays.
Referral program: "Give $5, Get $5" — customer shares a link, friend buys, both get $5 credit. Turns every happy customer into a sales channel.
VIP tier — "Mallow Club": Customers who spend $150+/year unlock free shipping, early access to new flavors, and a birthday gift. Ties into your subscription naturally.
Smile.io free tier: up to 200 monthly orders. Paid plan $49/mo. Start free.
Subscription platform for "Mallow of the Month": Square Online doesn't have native subscription billing. Use Bold Subscriptions (works with Square, $49/mo) or Recharge (if you ever move to Shopify). Both handle billing, dunning, and customer management automatically. The subscription product is the single highest-ROI change you can make — recurring revenue stabilizes cash flow immediately.
